PS-LPS First-Batch Experiment

This report compares ordinary LPS auto and local.auto against prediction-synchronized LPS (PS-LPS) variants that reuse the same selected support, kernel, degree, and chart-dimension policy, then tune only \(\lambda_{\mathrm{sync}}\) by CV.

Lambda grid: {0, 0.1, 1}. Sync neighbor size: 3. Best-method counts: LPS auto=0; LPS local.auto=0; PS-LPS auto=5; PS-LPS local.auto=9.

Truth RMSE

Lower Truth RMSE is better. Gray lines connect methods within a dataset.

Total Local GCV and CV RMSE

The plotted total local GCV is computed after synchronization, using the synchronized chart coefficients. It is therefore not the same quantity as total local GCV for independent LPS charts.

Each point is one PS-LPS lambda candidate on one dataset and one chart-dimension variant.

This synthetic-truth diagnostic asks whether the synchronized total local GCV of the selected PS-LPS fit is aligned with the final Truth RMSE. It should not be read as a deployable selection rule because Truth RMSE is unavailable on real data.

Synchronization Energy and Improvement

As an additional diagnostic, the next figure compares synchronization energy with the Truth-RMSE change relative to the corresponding ordinary LPS source fit. This helps check whether the empirical gains are plausibly connected to prediction synchronization.

Negative y-values mean PS-LPS improved over the ordinary LPS fit from which it inherited support size, kernel, degree, and chart dimension policy.

Pointwise Components

Positive c_i means the PS-LPS variant contributes more Truth-RMSE than ordinary LPS auto at that point. Colors show local degrees-of-freedom ratio.
